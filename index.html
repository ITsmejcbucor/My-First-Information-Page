About

Cherry Mobile is a Philippines-based mobile phone maker founded in 2008. The smartphone company imports phones from China and sells them under its brand. Cherry Mobile partnered with Google to become one of the first companies to launch the Android One initiative in Philippines.

Established by Maynard Ngu in 2009. In 2010, barely two years after it started, Cherry Mobile was voted IT Company of the Year in the 3rd CyberPress Awards, upstaging some of the country's technology giants. The company imports mobile phones manufactured by original design manufacturers in China and markets them under the Cherry Mobile brand. Most of Cherry Mobile's current lineup come with Wi-Fi, capacitive touch screens and run on the Android, Windows, and Windows Phone operating systems.


Founder CEO - Maynard Ngu, Age 37

Apart from being the first legal mobile phone brand with dual and triple Subscriber Identity Module (SIM) systems in the Philippines, Cherry Mobile also marketed the first Windows-enabled phone in the country as a result of an exclusive partnership with Microsoft.

Cherry Mobile recently launched Cherry Mobile M1, a product of such partnerships and the epitome of integrated tech design. Cherry Mobile partnered with Mediatek, one of the largest chip-design and system-on-chip solution for wireless communications companies in the world, to create the first local smartphone which utilizes a Deca-core processor. In addition to Mediatek’s Helio X20 deca-core processor, Cherry Mobile M1 boasts a Sony IMX230 Exmor RS smartphone camera sensor, which allows high speed image capturing and uncompromised photo quality.

Portfolio

At 37 years old, Maynard Ngu, the young CEO and visionary of Cherry Mobile has proven to many that a proudly local mobile brand can compete even against international tech titans. Through premium and affordable smartphones and devices that promise a seamless mobile experience, Cherry Mobile aims to hold onto its position as the leading local mobile phone brand in the country.

In 2009, seven years ago, entrepreneur Maynard Ngu had first considered breaking into the mobile phone scene by launching his own brand – Cherry Mobile, his brainchild. During that time, it seemed like an impossible feat as the market then was dominated by international tech giants such as Nokia and Blackberry.
Even with the surging “Proudly Pinoy” sentiments that urged consumers to support local brands, it posed a definite challenge.

In 2010, a mere year after it was founded, Cherry Mobile was recognized as the “IT Company of the Year” by the Cyber press Awards. It would snag another prestigious award, this time from Yugatech, as the “Top Selling Mobile Phone Brand” in 2013, beating industry heavyweights.

The impressive reception of Cherry Mobile pushed as well exponential growth within the company. Maynard’s once small operation, consisting of a lean team of 50, has grown to include around 750 employees.

“At the end of the day, my vision is that everybody will have a Cherry Mobile in their hands. We want to bring the Cherry Mobile experience to everybody, because Cherry Mobile is and will always be “everybody’s everyday device,” adds Maynard.

Creating Possibilities

Maynard assured quality in Cherry Mobile products, despite the continuous release of new phones, and it has proved credibility by partnering up with respected manufacturers and tech industry leaders such as Intel, Microsoft, Google, Facebook, Qualcomm, and MediaTek, among others in the development of its new phones.
Cherry Mobile recently launched Cherry Mobile M1, a product of such partnerships and the epitome of integrated tech design. Cherry Mobile partnered with Mediatek, one of the largest chip-design and system-on-chip solution for wireless communications companies in the world, to create the first local smartphone which utilizes a Deca-core processor. In addition to Mediatek’s Helio X20 deca-core processor, Cherry Mobile M1 boasts a Sony IMX230 Exmor RS smartphone camera sensor, which allows high speed image capturing and uncompromised photo quality.
“I think for any local brand, we should always aim to take the lead in pushing innovation.”
Not simply limiting Cherry Mobile’s influence in the mobile phone market, Maynard also diversified his business portfolio to include lifestyle devices such as the recent Cherry Mobile Explorer 2, the first ever Android-powered action camera. “Cherry Mobile is now exploring in developing our own apps as well as IoTs (or Internet of Things),” he reveals.

Giving Back to Communities

In gratitude of the massive growth of Cherry Mobile, Maynard is giving back his success through Cherry Mobile’s CSR programs and initiatives guided by the principles Cherry Cares (Customer, Natural Resources, Employees and Society. From providing school kits to building classrooms to underprivileged community through CherrySkwela, to planting seedlings for sustainable livelihood and many other acts of stewardship, Cherry Mobile aims to uplift the lives of the Filipinos and build a progressive and better place they deserve.



“This is our contribution to nation-building,” explains Maynard.

History

In 2013 - Cherry Mobile expanded its market by distributing their products in Thailand and in Myanmar.

In 2015 - Cosmic Technologies, along with rival mobile device company MyPhone, partnered with Google for the Android One initiative, both of them releasing their respective devices based on Google's reference designs using MediaTek's quad-core MT6582 system-on-chip.

On June 2015 - Cosmic Technologies announced that they will be launching two Windows Phone handsets preloaded with Windows 10 Mobile under their mobile phone brand, namely the Alpha Prime 4 and the Alpha Prime 5, which was released in the second half of 2015. Both devices came with a 1.1 GHz quad core Qualcomm Snapdragon 210, support for WCDMA 900/2100, FDD-LTE B1/B3/B5/B7, and a 2 megapixel front-facing camera. The Alpha Prime 4 will have a 4-inch screen, a 5 megapixel main camera, 4 GB of ROM, and 512 MB of RAM, while the Alpha Prime 5 will have a 5-inch screen, 8 megapixel main camera, 8 GB of ROM and 1 GB of RAM.

In 2016 - Cherry Mobile established official presence in Europe. It started distributing its products in Germany.

On October 2018 - Cherry Mobile Flare S7 series' specifications and design were leaked ahead of their launch. Its design for the S7 Deluxe and Plus series were using the long notch from the iPhone XS.

Cherry Mobile was placed under a bigger brand known as Cherry in 2020, along with other brands by Cosmic Technologies including Cherry Prepaid, Cherryroam, Cherry Home, and Cherry Pet.

Today many product lines which Cherry Mobile is currently developing, from household, cleaning devices, electric cooking devices and home security products. Cherry Mobile extending its venture to other electronic products and devices.
